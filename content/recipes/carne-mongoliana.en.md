{{< lead >}}
Strips of top sirloin stir-fried in a sweet-savory soy, garlic, and ginger sauce, Mongolian Beef style.
{{< /lead >}}

# Ingredients
{{< keyword icon="user" >}} 2-3 servings {{< /keyword >}}

### For the beef
* **400 g** top sirloin, cut into thin strips 5 mm thick and 5-7 cm long
* **2 tbsp** cornstarch
* **1 tbsp** light soy sauce
* **1/2 tbsp** Chinese rice wine (Shaoxing) or dry sherry (optional but ideal)
* **1 tsp** toasted sesame oil
* **1 pinch** ground white pepper

### For the sauce
* **3 tbsp** dark soy sauce (if you don't have it, use regular soy sauce + 1 extra tsp sugar)
* **2 tbsp** light soy sauce
* **2 tbsp** dark brown sugar (or grated panela)
* **1/2 cup** water
* **1 tsp** cornstarch dissolved in 1 tbsp cold water
* **1 tbsp** oyster sauce (optional, adds umami)
* **1 tsp** rice vinegar or mild white vinegar

### For stir-frying
* **3-4** garlic cloves, finely minced
* **1 piece (2 cm)** fresh ginger, finely chopped or julienned
* **4-5** green onions (white and green parts), cut into 5 cm pieces
* Neutral vegetable oil for frying (ideally peanut or canola)

# Step by step
{{< keyword icon="clock" >}} 45 min (includes marinating) {{< /keyword >}}

### 1. Marinate the beef (15-30 min)
1. Cut the top sirloin into thin strips, as perpendicular to the grain as possible.
2. In a bowl, mix the beef with the [For the beef](#for-the-beef) ingredients.
3. Refrigerate while you prepare the rest.

### 2. Make the sauce
1. In a bowl, mix the [For the sauce](#for-the-sauce) ingredients.
2. Set aside.

### 3. Cook the beef
1. Heat 3-4 tbsp of vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat until very hot (without smoking).
2. Add the beef (in batches if needed) and stir-fry quickly, just until browned on the outside (1-2 min). Don't overcook.
3. Remove the beef and drain on paper towels.

### 4. Stir-fry the aromatics
1. In the same wok (remove excess oil), add a splash of fresh oil.
2. Sauté the garlic and ginger for 30 seconds until fragrant.
3. Add the sauce and cook over medium-high heat until it bubbles and thickens slightly (1 min).

### 5. Bring it together
1. Return the beef to the wok and mix well to coat with the sauce.
2. Add the green onions and stir for 30 more seconds, until slightly softened but still bright in color.

# Notes / Tips
- Serve immediately over jasmine rice or fluffy white rice, ideally with a touch of sesame seeds and fresh chopped green onion.
- Cutting the beef thin and against the grain is key to keeping the sirloin tender.
- Coating the beef in cornstarch acts like Chinese "velveting": it tenderizes and protects the meat during cooking.
- You can add stir-fried vegetables like broccoli or bell peppers to make it more of a full meal, but the classic recipe doesn't include them.
