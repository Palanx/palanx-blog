{{< lead >}}
A four-bean stew with pork and chorizo, smoked with cacho de cabra chili and thickened with its own blended paste.
{{< /lead >}}

# Ingredients
{{< keyword icon="user" >}} Large batch: ~750 g beans + 900 g pork (makes a lot, freezes well) {{< /keyword >}}

### Beans
* **188 g** black beans
* **188 g** red beans
* **188 g** chickpeas
* **188 g** "hallado" beans (a Chilean bean variety, no direct English name)

### Meat
* **900 g** pork tenderloin, cubed
* **6** grilling chorizo sausages, casings removed and crumbled

### Vegetables (sofrito)
* **2** large onions, diced
* **2** bell peppers, diced
* **2** large carrots, diced
* **2** whole green onions, sliced

### Heat and smoke
* **2** cacho de cabra (red chili), dried and wood-smoked
* **3** small hot chilies (guindillas, optional, makes it quite spicy)

### Seasoning
* **5 tablespoons** panela (raw cane sugar)
* **1** head of garlic, chopped
* **1/2 tablespoon** cumin
* **1 tablespoon** dried cilantro
* Chopped fresh cilantro (to taste)
* Chopped chives (optional, to taste)
* Pepper (to taste)
* Salt (to taste)

# Step by step
{{< keyword icon="clock" >}} 24 h soaking + ~1 h cooking {{< /keyword >}}

### 1. Soak the beans (24 h ahead)
Soak the [Beans](#beans) in cold water for 24 hours. When you're ready to cook, drain them in a large colander and rinse.

### 2. Vegetable sofrito
In a wok, add a generous amount of oil and heat over high heat. Sauté the onion and green onion. Once the onion is golden, add the cacho de cabra chilies, the guindillas, the bell pepper, and the carrot, and cook until the vegetables soften. Add the panela, cumin, and dried cilantro, plus the fresh cilantro or chives if you want, season with salt and pepper to taste, and sauté until everything is well mixed. Set aside in a bowl.

### 3. Meat and chorizo
In the same wok, add another generous amount of oil and heat over high heat. Add the pork and the chorizo, season with salt and pepper to taste, and sauté until fully cooked.

### 4. Load the pressure cooker
Add the beans and 2/3 of the reserved vegetable sofrito to the pressure cooker. From the wok with the meat, take out 1/3 of the meat and chorizo, drain it well of its juice, and put it in the bowl together with the remaining 1/3 of sofrito; that whole bowl (sofrito + meat without juice) is what gets set aside for later. The remaining 2/3 of meat and chorizo left in the wok, together with all the fat and juice, goes straight into the pot. Add boiling water to cover the ingredients, so the chorizo juice mixes into that water, and taste the water: it should be salty, but not overly so, since the beans will absorb that salt as they cook.

### 5. Pressure cook
Once the pot reaches full pressure and starts releasing steam, lower to medium heat and cook for 30 min at 10 PSI (the meat setting).

### 6. Thicken and finish
Once it's done and the pot is safe to open, scoop out about 3 ladlefuls of the mixture, making sure to grab the chilies (the cacho de cabra and the guindillas) so they end up in that separate container, since they're going to be blended. Add one more tablespoon of just the liquid. Blend everything with an immersion blender until it becomes a smooth paste, then stir it back into the pot. Add the reserved 1/3 of vegetables and meat from the bowl. Mix everything together and it's done.

# Notes / Tips
- The 24-hour soak is essential, skip it and the beans will stay tough.
- Setting aside 1/3 of the sofrito and meat without the chorizo fat is what keeps some texture at the end, not everything gets lost in the blended paste.
