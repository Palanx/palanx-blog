{{< lead >}}
Papaya jam with ginger and lime, slow-cooked until it reaches the perfect consistency.
{{< /lead >}}

# Ingredients
{{< keyword icon="user" >}} For 500 g of seeded papaya {{< /keyword >}}
* **500 g** peeled papaya, seeds removed
* **180 g** sugar
* **1 tablespoon** lime juice
* **1 teaspoon** fresh ginger, finely chopped (or grated)

# Step by step
{{< keyword icon="clock" >}} ~30 min {{< /keyword >}}

### Peel the papaya and remove the seeds
1. Peel the whole papaya with a sharp, serrated knife. If the papaya is cut open first it loses firmness, which makes peeling a pain.
2. The inner pulp is full of seeds. Using a metal strainer to press the pulp against is an easy way to separate the seeds.

### Precise cooking
1. Add the papaya, sugar, lime juice, and ginger to the pot.
2. Let it rest **20–30 min** to macerate (it releases juice).
3. Set the heat to **medium-low**.
4. Once it reaches a gentle simmer, cook for **25–30 min**.
5. Stir every 3–4 min.
6. At the **22–25 min** mark, do the cold plate test: it should run slowly and not set stiff.

# Notes / Tips
- Take it off the heat while it's still a bit runnier than you want, it thickens as it cools.
- Blend with an immersion blender at the end for a smoother consistency.
