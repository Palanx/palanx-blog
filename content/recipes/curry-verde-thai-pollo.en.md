{{< lead >}}
Chicken cooked in a creamy Thai green curry, with stir-fried vegetables and just the right hit of acid and herbs.
{{< /lead >}}

# Ingredients
{{< keyword icon="user" >}} For 850 g of chicken {{< /keyword >}}

### Chicken
* **850 g** chicken, cut into 3-4 cm pieces

### Vegetables
* **1** zucchini, in thick half-moons (1-1.5 cm)
* **1** eggplant, in medium cubes
* **1** bell pepper, in strips
* **100 g** green beans (optional)

### Aromatics
* **3** garlic cloves, finely chopped
* **1 piece (2 cm)** fresh ginger, grated

### Herbs
* **1 handful (15-20 leaves)** Thai basil **or** regular basil + crushed fennel seeds + whole star anise (substitution)

### For the curry
* **3½-4 tablespoons** green curry paste
* **1 can (400 ml)** coconut milk (unshaken), used in two parts: **¾ cup** at the start and the rest later on
* **2 tablespoons** fish sauce
* **1½ teaspoons** sugar
* **1-1½ tablespoons** lime or lemon juice
* Neutral oil (optional, in case no oil separates from the coconut milk)

# Step by step
{{< keyword icon="clock" >}} ~40 min {{< /keyword >}}

### Mise en place
Do this before turning on the heat, so you're not scrambling later: cut the chicken, get the [Vegetables](#vegetables) ready, chop the [Aromatics](#aromatics), prep the [Herbs](#herbs), and have the curry paste, coconut milk, fish sauce, sugar, and lime or lemon within reach.

### 1. Split the coconut milk fat
Heat a wide pot or wok over medium-high heat and add ¾ cup of coconut milk. Don't stir at first. After 2-4 minutes the liquid should thicken and a shiny oil should appear on the surface. If you don't see oil, add 1 tablespoon of neutral oil and keep going the same way.

### 2. Fry the curry paste
Add 3½-4 tablespoons of green curry paste and stir constantly for 1-2 minutes. Add the garlic, ginger, and star anise (if using the substitution). You should get a strong, herbal aroma, a more intense green color, and a slightly green oil. If it dries out, add a splash of coconut milk, never water.

### 3. Sear the chicken in the curry
Add all the raw chicken and mix well to coat it in the curry. Lower the heat to medium and cook for 2-3 minutes: the chicken should lose its pink color on the outside without browning heavily, and the bottom of the pot shouldn't burn. Don't remove the chicken or add salt yet.

### 4. Build the curry
Add the rest of the coconut milk and stir gently. Keep the heat at medium and cook for 12-15 minutes without a hard boil, until you get a gentle simmer, a creamy sauce, and evenly cooked chicken.

### 5. Add the vegetables
Add the eggplant and green beans (if using) first and cook for 3 minutes. Then add the zucchini and bell pepper, and cook for 2-4 more minutes, until the vegetables are tender but still have some bite.

### 6. Adjust the seasoning
Add 2 tablespoons of fish sauce and 1½ teaspoons of sugar, stir, and taste. If it needs salt, add more fish sauce; if it's too strong, add more coconut milk; if it's flat, add an extra pinch of sugar.

### 7. The acid
Turn off the heat, add 1 tablespoon of lime or lemon juice, stir, and taste. If needed, add ½ tablespoon more. The citrus shouldn't taste sour, it should just lift the aroma.

### 8. Final herbs
With the heat off, add the basil (and the fennel seeds if using the substitution), stir gently, cover the pot for 1 minute, and remove the star anise.

### 9. Serve
Serve hot, with jasmine rice. The curry should flow, look glossy, and coat the rice without drowning it.

# Notes / Tips
- Signs it turned out well: a fresh aroma when you lift the lid, green oil on the surface, juicy chicken, and a creamy (not watery) sauce.
- If the curry dries out at any point, fix it with a splash of coconut milk, never water.
