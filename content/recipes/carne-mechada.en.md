{{< lead >}}
Seared short ribs, drowned in red wine and vegetables until they fall apart tender on their own. Nothing fancy, it's the one I always make and it slaps.
{{< /lead >}}

# Ingredients
{{< keyword icon="user" >}} 6-8 servings {{< /keyword >}}

### Sofrito
* **1** onion, diced
* **1** leek, sliced into rounds
* **1** red bell pepper, diced
* **1** green bell pepper, diced
* **3** carrots, diced
* **5** garlic cloves, minced

### Reduction
* **400-500 ml** red wine (just eyeball it, don't overthink the exact amount)
* **250 ml** water with a beef bouillon cube dissolved in it

### Beef
* **1.5 kg** short ribs (boneless), trimmed of excess fat

### Seasoning
* Table salt
* Freshly ground pepper

# Step by step
{{< keyword icon="clock" >}} Pressure cooker 45 min / regular pot 3 h {{< /keyword >}}

### 1. Reduction
Put the wine and the bouillon water in a pot over medium-high heat and let it reduce until it's down to half the original volume. Once it's ready, set that pot aside and forget about it for a while.

### 2. Sear the beef
While the wine reduces, get on with searing the beef, no point just standing there staring at the pot. Cut the meat into chunks about 3 inches long, **parallel to the grain** (cut it against the grain and it'll fall apart in the pot, and then you've really messed it up). Heat another pot over medium-high heat with a few good glugs of oil, that same oil gets reused for the sofrito later, so don't toss it. Add as many pieces of beef as fit, salt and pepper on every side, and sear it well all around. As each piece gets seared, move it straight into the pot with the wine.

### 3. Sofrito
In that same pot, using the same oil you seared the beef in, throw in the whole [Sofrito](#sofrito). Salt and pepper to taste. Sauté for about 10 min, until softened.

### 4. Bring it together
Add the sautéed sofrito into the pot with the wine and beef. Stir a bit so the meat and vegetables mix well and sit below the liquid line.

### 5. Cook it
- **Pressure cooker:** 45 min at 10 PSI (the meat setting).
- **Regular pot:** low heat for 3 hours (can't remember if lid on or off, try it yourself and let me know).

### 6. Finish
Carefully pull the beef out and set it aside. Blend everything left in the pot with an immersion blender until it turns into a thick mush. Add the beef back in whole, don't shred it, the idea is that it keeps its shape and each person shreds it themselves right on their plate. And that's it, done.

# Notes / Tips
- I eyeballed the wine, somewhere between 400 and 500 ml, no need to be precise about it.
- Use the dead time wisely: while the wine reduces, you can already be searing the beef and sautéing the veggies.
- Cut the beef against the grain instead of parallel and it'll fall apart in the pot, watch out for that.
